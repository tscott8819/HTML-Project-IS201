    • I love backcountry skiing! Backcountry skiing is a great way to get some exercise in but also have lots of fun on the way back down.

    There are some risks to backcountry skiing. One must be very aware of avalanches at all times. You have to constantly be checking the conditions, you have to dig pits to find weak layers in the snow, and you have to ski at the right time of day.

      • Conditions: The conditions have to be just right. Avalanches happen generally on slopes greater than 30 degrees. Avalanches also generally happen when there is softer snow on top of harder snow.
      • Digging Pits: Skiers will often dig pits through multiple layers of snow to test the different layers. A skier (while standing in the pit) can put their shovel on top of the snow and tap it to see if any agrivation will cause a break in one of the layers. If there is a break, the snow is not safe.
      • Timing: Skiers try to ski in the morning as it is cooler. When snow has been baked in the sun all day, it can make the top layer softer with hard layers underneath, making it easier for entire snow slabs to break off and cause an avalanche.
